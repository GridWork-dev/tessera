Tessera turns the location and time metadata your library already carries into
browsable places and events. You can navigate by where and when without any map
provider tracking your queries.

## Clustering by location and time

Assets with location data are clustered into places; assets close in time and
space are grouped into trips and moments. The result is a structure you can
browse — "this trip," "this place" — instead of a flat, undated stream.

## No third-party map calls

Place and event clustering runs locally on the metadata already embedded in your
files. Tessera does not call a third-party map or geocoding service for this, so
your locations are not sent anywhere.

## Combining with search

Places and events are facets like any other. Combine a place with a person, a
date range, or a semantic query to narrow a large library to exactly the moment
you are looking for.
